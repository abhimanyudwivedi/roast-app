# Roast Techniques (craft reference)

Distilled from Indian roast-comedy analysis. This is **craft** — how to build a roast that lands. The real named comedians in the source are examples for learning technique, **not personas to build and not people to roast in the app**. Learn the mechanism; write original material aimed at the user's own habits.

## The core formula

**[Setup A: their claim] + [Unexpected B: their reality] = punchline.**
Two true-ish things collided so the gap is the joke.
- "World-class plan + zero execution = your execution's visa got rejected."
- "5 hours researching productivity + 0 minutes working = you optimized the runway, never took off."

## Techniques

- **Irony / self-deprecation** — spotlight the gap between what they say and what they do.
- **Wordplay / pun** — twist a word ("wedding ring" → "ring light").
- **Hyperbole** — exaggerate the behaviour to absurdity.
- **Juxtaposition** — smash two mismatched images together.
- **Satire** — mock the *pattern*, never the person.
- **Callback** — reuse their earlier excuse against them ("Day 4 of 'starting Monday'").

## The "motivational slap" (the redirect) — ADAPTED for mental health

The source lands the redirect with **guilt** ("you have AC and internet, why cry burnout?"). We keep the *contrast → call-to-action* shape but land on **agency, not shame** — guilt harms vulnerable users.

- **Contrast:** their excuse vs. what's actually doable.
- **Redirect:** one concrete, small action, right now.
- **Empower:** "you can do this," tied to their `goal`.

**DO:** "You've been 'planning' for a month. The plan doesn't need you — the doing does. One rep today. You've got this."
**DON'T:** shame, humiliate, or "someone had it worse, so you're pathetic." Motivate, don't crush.

## Intensity (maps to persona filter levels)

- **unfiltered / savage:** sharper contrast, faster punchline, minimal cushion.
- **semi / medium:** punchline with visible warmth.
- **filtered / low:** more wit than sting, quick to the empowering turn.

## Hard line (see roast-persona safety rules)

No religion, caste, communal, political, or national-sentiment material, about anyone. In India that's a legal/arrest risk, not a style choice. Roasts stay on personal habits and excuses.

## What NOT to lift from the source

The specific roasts about named real comedians are illustrations of technique only. Do not reproduce them as content, do not build those people as personas, do not roast users "as" them. Write original, on-user material.
